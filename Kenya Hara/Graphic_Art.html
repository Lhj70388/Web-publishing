Entry for the Beijing Summer Olympic Games Symbol
Design Competition (2003)

These posters were produced for the annual paper show held by Takeo,
a paper trading company. The posters on display date from 1990
through 1994, showing Hara's experiments with form and texture
that date back to the earliest days of his career. In the printing
process, he combined textures of things like gold foil, fruit skin
and such with abstract forms with three-dimensional shadows.

LIFE Posters (1998)

Graphic designer Takenobu Igarashi and Hara worked on a series of
five graphics on the theme of Life. Hara made sketch after sketch,
based on the images they get when they sense the tension and
vines and stems. The images are tangible, but the shapes are not a
island of Shikoku. Reproduction of anything in particular, they
are but an abstract.

NOH (2017)

The "Twenty-three Posters 1981/2017" project by UCLA and Waseda
University revisited a posters project from 36 years earlier.
Kenya Hara was responsible for "Noh," and his poster illustrates
the emergence of an image on a Noh mask. Below the poster are
three rough sketches.

A Book (1996)

The cover design of the magazine, A Book has a single visual, and is
devoid of any superfluous information. Illustrator Yoshitaka
Mizutani, given to producing illustrations with awesome precision,
helps turn Hara's sketches into precise, sophisticated images.
