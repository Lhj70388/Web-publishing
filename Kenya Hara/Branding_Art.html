Branding




煮葉 TEASURE (2017)

TEASURE opened in Beijing in 2017 as a café space for enjoying
traditional Chinese teas in an informal setting. Hara Design
Institute designed logos, visual identity elements, and packaging,
also handled the space design, design of tableware. Hara took a
typeface that had emerged naturally in the course of his work,
and extended it to include Chinese characters-traditional.

Centennial Celebration of the
Establishment of Meiji Jingu
(2017)

The year 2020 will be the 100th anniversary of the enshrinement of Meiji
Shrine. We created the symbol mark for commemorating the event.
It consists of two circles, with the golden one symbolizing the
spirits of the enshrined deities and the green one symbolizing the
forest around the shrine. The symbol mark connecting these two circles
is a celebration of the centennial visualized.

Tsutaya Shoten (2011)

The Daikanyama Tsutaya Shoten (bookstore) project is symbolized by
the logo renovation. We replaced the Roman letters with the Chinese
characters (kanji) for Tsutaya Shoten:蔦屋書店. The logotype is concise
and easy to read. We developed the VI system by separating the logo
into an arrangement of dots.

Mori Building (2000)

Mori Building is a corporation dedicated to creating "Vertical Garden Cities,"
an urban developer formalizing that vision in Japan and other parts of Asia.
This program ranges from creating the symbol mark to continuous quality
improvement, including updating the signs on existing buildings. This mark,
symbolizes the viewpoint of Mori Building, whose goal is to help create highly
efficient highrise “garden cities”.
