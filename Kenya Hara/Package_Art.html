Packaging




Hakkaisan (2016)

This bottle represents the essence of Japanese saké. It features a label
with calligraphy by Kyuyo Ishikawa. The bottle is designed with a
bold look befitting the highest-quality saké in the history of
Hakkaisan.

Hakkin (2002)

This is the shape of the bottle that came out of a package design request
from Obuse-do, in central Japan's Nagano Prefecture, for their Hakkin
[Platinum] sake. We crystallized the sake's identity with the label on
the neck, made of hand-torn Japanese paper from Tokushima, on the
island of Shikoku.

Teasure (2017)

These are designs for the packages of tea leaves sold in the cafe. The
designs combine the same typeface as the TEASURE logotype with
illustrations of tea leaves dancing in hot water.

Obuse-do (1998)

Obukuri-bin is a product catalog produced twice a year to vividly
depict Obusedo's chestnut confections. Each edition communicates
the attractions of the Obuse area in the current season.
The catalog is available at Obusedo outlets.
