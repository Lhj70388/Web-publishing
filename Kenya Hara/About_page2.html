About




Kenya Hara’s artistic process begins with a strong conceptual foundation. He often
explores deep philosophical ideas and concepts, drawing inspiration from nature,
Japanese culture, and therelationship between objects and people. He seeks to
create works that provoke thought and evoke emotions.

Hara experiments with a wide range of materials to bring his ideas to life. He explores
traditional Japanese materials and craftsmanship techniques, as well as modern
materials and technologies. Through material exploration, he aims to find the most
suitable medium to express his concept effectively

History

In 1992 he founded Hara Design Institute working in the whole field of design from advertising
to branding, exhibit design, sign systems, packaging, products, and books. His design communicates
the essence through the essential: The profound meaning of each reality through symbolic visual
solutions that have their origin in Japanese traditional culture.

Over the years, he has worked for major Japanese clients including Art Setouchi Triennale, Kenzo,
Matsuya department stores, the Nagasaki Prefectural Art Museum, Tsutaya Books, and the
Umeda Hospital. In 1998 he also designed the opening and closing ceremonies of Nagano
1998 Winter Olympics.



In 2001 he was appointed Muji’s art director by Ikko Tanaka, a master of Japanese graphic
design who was its former art director. Since then, he both develops communication design and
helps the company to formulate an integrated vision. He curates Muji’s advertising, apps,
commercials, exhibitions, magazine, and packaging following the original concept that “sometimes
simplicity surpasses splendor.” Under his art direction, Muji has become an international symbol
of aesthetic and conceptual quality.

Kenya Hara’s contributions to the design field have earned him international recognition.
His minimalist approach, emphasis on design philosophy, and collaborations with major brands
have had a significant impact on the world of design and have made him a respected figure in
the industry.
